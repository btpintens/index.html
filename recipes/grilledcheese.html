Grilled Cheese

In many British Colonies a toasty bread and melty cheese combo is referred to as a Cheese Toasty. But, this is America, and we call it a Grilled Cheese. It's about as American as apple pie and babies with guns. As with both those things, it's best not to overthink the process; let your God-given intuition guide the way. And if you can't digest dairy why the hell are you looking up cheese recipes? Move along.

Ingredients

  • bread - ideally something fluffy and soft, like a sourdough
  • butter
  • cheese - an assortment is recommended, with emphasis on american and cheddar

Steps

  1. turn your burner to a medium/medium low setting
  2. butter one side of bread and place the buttered side down in the pan
  3. while your bread is toasting, place 2-3 layers of your cheese on top of the bread
  4. while the bread continues to toast, and the cheese starts to melt, prepare the second piece of bread by buttering just one side of it
  5. place the "dry" (aka not buttered side) of the bread on top of the bread and cheese platform you have built in your pan
  6. now is a great time to check the status of your toast. using your preferred utensil (knife, spatula, finger) take a peek at the underside of the base layer. depending on the level of heat, and the speed in which you've assembled, the underside should start to turn a beatiful golden hue. if this hasn't started to happen, give it another minute. maybe turn the heat up a little. but remember: there is no need to rush the process.
  7. once the bottom layer of bread has achieved your preferred level of brown/golden/burnt it's time to flip the whole thing over. use a spatula, google the plate trick, or throw caution to the wind and flip it in the pan like you see done in the movies.
  8. sit back and wait for this final toast. it should take anywhere from 3-5 minutes.
  9. now eat it. i like a diagonal cut but maybe your a Philistine and don't give a hoot about aesthetics. you do you.
