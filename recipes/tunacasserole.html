Tuna Casserole

Forget about the grilled cheese being iconically american and shift your focus to the art of casserole. Specifically, the Tuna Casserole. It doesn't make sense. It shouldn't make sense. But, here we are.

Ingredients

  • noodles: elbow, rotini, shells, etc.
  • canned tuna
  • shredded cheddar
  • frozen peas
  • potato chips: brand doesn't matter but don't buy an overthought kettle cooked, flavored, "thick", etc. it's important you stick to basics. the cheaper the probably better.
  • mayonnaise

The Process

  1. preheat the oven to 375
  2. cook your noodles per box instructions, BUT, slightly undercook them. the noodles will continue to cook once they go in the oven and we want to avoid a mushy nood.
  3. in a mixing bowl combine tuna (drained of liquid), frozen peas (no need to thaw), mayo, and par-cooked noodles
  4. pour into a 9x12 bake dish and cover with shredded cheddar, crushed potato chips, and tinfoil
  5. bake (while covered) for 35 minutes
  6. remove tinfoil and continue to bake for another 30 minutes. as the end approaches the cheese will bubble and the chips will become golden.
